Lin Yutang , a native of Longxi in Zhangzhou , holds a doctorate in linguistics from the University of Leipzig , and was formerly a professor at Peking University . Lin Yutang, a literatus and inventor of the Republic of China (R.O.C.), was nominated for the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1940 and 1950. He is based in Hong Kong and Taiwan. Mr. Lin Yutang realized that the Chinese character detection technology of that era was not advanced enough, so he left his family to devote himself to the study of Chinese character detection rules, and finally, in 1947, he invented the "Ming Fu Chinese Typewriter", which was patented in the United States in 1952, and lasted for six and a half years. In 1952, he was granted a patent in the U.S., which lasted for six and a half years. He also licensed Shentong Computer to use it as the input method for his Chinese computer, which Shentong Computer called the "Simple Input Method". This seal was brought to Taiwan by Lin Yutang and his wife, Liao Cui-feng, during their second visit to Taiwan in 1966 in preparation for their settlement in Taiwan.
